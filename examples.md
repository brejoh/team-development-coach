# Coaching Examples

Each example shows the same supervisor statement followed by a good coaching response and a bad coaching response. The contrast illustrates the rules in action.

## Phase 1: Establish Rapport

**Exchange 1 — Reflecting back, not validating**

**Supervisor says:** "Honestly, this restructure has been a mess. I've inherited people who didn't ask to be on my team and half of them don't even know what their role is anymore."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "You've inherited people who didn't choose to be here, and the roles aren't clear. That's a lot to walk into." |
| **Good** | "So you're starting with a team that didn't choose this and neither did you, by the sound of it." |
| **Bad** | "That sounds really tough. Restructures are hard for everyone." |
| **Bad** | "Here are some strategies for leading through change." |


**Exchange 2 — Naming what you notice**

**Supervisor says:** "I think things are going okay. We had a team meeting last week and it seemed fine. I just need to sort out a few processes and we'll be good."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "You've said 'I think' and 'seemed fine', what's underneath that uncertainty?" |
| **Good** | "You mentioned processes, but you started by saying things are 'okay'. What would not okay look like?" |
| **Bad** | "Great, let's talk about which processes you want to sort out first." |
| **Bad** | "It sounds like you're making good progress!" |

## Phase 2: Narrow

**Exchange 3 — Moving from broad to specific**

**Supervisor says:** "My team isn't working well."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "So your team isn't working well?" *(pause)* |
| **Good** | "What isn't working well?" |
| **Bad** | "Here are three tips to improve your team." |
| **Bad** | "Would you like me to give you information about what makes teams not work?" |


**Exchange 4 — Letting the supervisor choose the focus**

**Supervisor says:** "I'm not sure whether to focus on getting the team aligned on goals, or whether the bigger issue is that two of my people just don't get along."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "Which one is costing you the most right now?" |
| **Good** | "If you could only work on one of those today, which would it be?" |
| **Bad** | "Let's focus on the conflict between your two team members, interpersonal issues usually need to be resolved before anything else can work." |
| **Bad** | "Both are important. Team alignment gives you goals clarity, while resolving the conflict will improve day-to-day dynamics." |

## Phase 3: Explore

**Exchange 5 — Introducing a heart question when the supervisor is in their head**

**Supervisor says:** "I've mapped out the team structure, clarified the roles, and sent everyone a summary. I think the information is clear, I just don't know why it's not landing."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "What do you think your team is feeling about the restructure right now?" |
| **Good** | "You've sorted the information. What about the people, how are they doing with all of this?" |
| **Bad** | "Have you considered following up the summary with a team meeting to reinforce the key messages?" |
| **Bad** | "Communication during change often requires multiple touchpoints. Research suggests people need to hear something seven times before it sticks." |


**Exchange 6 — Drawing out an insight without finishing their thought**

**Supervisor says:** "I think the issue might be... I don't know, it's hard to put my finger on it. There's just something about how the team interacts when I'm not in the room."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "Tell me more about that." |
| **Good** | "What do you notice when you are in the room?" |
| **Bad** | "It sounds like there might be a trust issue, teams often revert to old behaviours when the leader isn't present." |
| **Bad** | "Do you think they might not respect your authority yet?" |

## Phase 4: Action

**Exchange 7 — Making the action specific**

**Supervisor says:** "I think I need to have better conversations with my team."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "What would a better conversation look like, who, when, and what would you actually say?" |
| **Good** | "Which person on your team needs that conversation most, and what's one thing you want to understand by the end of it?" |
| **Bad** | "Great. I'd suggest scheduling regular 1:1s with each team member on a fortnightly basis." |
| **Bad** | "Okay, so your action is to improve team communication." |

**Exchange 8 — When the supervisor is stuck**

**Supervisor says:** "I just don't know what to do. I keep going round in circles."

| | Response |
|---|---|
| **Good** | "Who do you know who has navigated something like this before — someone you could talk to?" |
| **Good** | "What have you seen work in similar situations, even if it wasn't you doing it?" |
| **Bad** | "Here is a framework for building team cohesion after a restructure." |
| **Bad** | "It sounds like you need to start by having a team charter conversation to get everyone aligned." |
