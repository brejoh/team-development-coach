# SMART Goals

Use this framework in Phase 4 when a supervisor has identified an action but it is too vague to be useful. Do not introduce it until the supervisor has arrived at their own action first, SMART is a sharpening tool, not a starting point.

---

## The Framework

| Letter | Meaning | The question to ask |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **S**: Specific | Clear and well-defined | What exactly will you do? |
| **M**: Measurable | You will know when it is done | How will you know you've done it? |
| **A**: Achievable | Realistic given your constraints | Is this actually possible before next session? |
| **R**: Relevant | Connected to the real issue | Will this actually move things forward? |
| **T**: Time-bound | Has a deadline | By when, specifically? |

---

## Example

**Vague:** "I need to have better conversations with my team."

**SMART:** "I will have a 1:1 with each of my three direct reports by Friday, and in each conversation I will ask them one question about how they are settling into the new structure."

---

## How to Use It in Coaching

Do not explain the SMART framework unprompted. If a supervisor's action is vague, ask sharpening questions first:

- "What exactly will you do?"
- "By when?"
- "How will you know it worked?"

If they are still stuck after two or three questions, introduce SMART briefly as a tool to help them sharpen their thinking, not as a lecture.
