# Team Formation: Tuckman's Model

Use this framework when a supervisor is trying to understand why their team feels the way it does, or when they can't articulate what stage they're at. Post-restructure teams often get reset back to Forming or Storming regardless of how long the individuals have worked together.

## The Four Stages

### Forming
The team has just come together. People are polite, cautious, and uncertain. They are figuring out the rules, the leader, and their place in the group. Conflict is avoided. Energy is low or performatively positive.

**What the supervisor might say:** "Everyone seems fine, but I'm not sure they're being honest with me."

**Supervisor's role:** Be visible. Be clear about direction. Create safety for people to speak up.

### Storming
The politeness wears off. Conflict emerges, between people, about roles, about ways of working, or about the supervisor's authority. This is normal and necessary. Teams that skip storming rarely reach high performance.

**What the supervisor might say:** "Two of my people clash constantly" or "I feel like my authority is being tested."

**Supervisor's role:** Hold the space. Do not smooth over conflict, help the team work through it. Stay consistent.

### Norming
The team starts to find its rhythm. Roles are clearer. Trust is building. People are more willing to collaborate and challenge each other constructively.

**What the supervisor might say:** "Things feel calmer but I want to make sure we don't get too comfortable."

**Supervisor's role:** Reinforce the behaviours that got them here. Begin shifting ownership to the team.

### Performing
The team operates with high trust, clear purpose, and real autonomy. The supervisor's role shifts from managing to enabling.

**What the supervisor might say:** "The team is running well, I'm not sure what my job is anymore."

**Supervisor's role:** Get out of the way. Focus on removing obstacles and developing individuals.

## Post-Restructure Note

Restructures reset teams. Even experienced people who have worked together before will cycle back to Forming when the structure, the purpose, or the leader changes. Do not assume the team is further along than it is.
